Postgraduate Course: Muslim Africa in Global Perspective (PG) (IMES11118)

Course Outline

Credit level (Normal year taken)SCQF Level 11 (Postgraduate) AvailabilityAvailable to all students
SCQF Credits20 ECTS Credits10
SummaryThis course is organized around the problem of Africa's marginalisation within studies of Islam and globalisation. By drawing on theories, ethnographies and documentary films, the course explores the globality of Muslim Africa. In doing so, it introduces students to the key concepts and debates within the field of Islam in Africa. With its specific focus on case studies from West Africa, this course aims to develop a deeper understanding rather than providing a general survey of Muslim societies in the continent.
Course description This course comprises two streams designed to achieve two parallel objectives. The first stream introduces students to the key themes, concepts and debates within the field of Islam in Africa through ethnographic studies in Senegal, Mali, Niger and Nigeria. The thematic topics explored by the course include, but are not limited to Islamic literature, epistemologies and institutions of Islamic learning, Sufism, reform movements, new media, feminisation of religious authority, and African youth cultures. These discussions will be supported by documentary films. By the end of this course, students will have a solid grounding in the anthropology of Islam in West Africa and an in-depth understanding of ethnographic methodology.

In parallel to the thematic discussions, the second stream introduces students to the theories of globalisation developed from within Africa, more specifically Muslim Africa. Students will gain a more critical and nuanced understanding of Africa's place in the world by studying diverse frameworks such as Afromodernity, Ajamisation and Afropolitanism. Students will at the same time emerge from the course with enhanced knowledge of Muslim Africa's historical and contemporary links to the rest of the Muslim world.

The course is organized in weekly two-hour sessions (jointly taught with UG students) which will be a combination of lecture and discussion-based seminar. It will also include two PG-only sessions. Students are expected to engage with their learning through in-class activities such as presentations, discussions and group exercises. They are also encouraged to attend extra-curricular events organized by the Alwaleed Centre, IMES, and the Centre of African Studies to complement the knowledge gained in the course.

Learning Outcomes
On completion of this course, the student will be able to:
  1. Consolidate in-depth knowledge of Islam in Africa, its historical and contemporary connectivity to the rest of the Muslim world and demonstrate a solid understanding of the key concepts, debates, and issues within the interdisciplinary field of Islam in Africa
  2. Think critically about the Eurocentric conceptualisations of globalisation and reframe globality based on theories, ethnographies and films produced in and on Africa
  3. Critically review and analyse the anthropological literature on West Africa and apply anthropological approaches and ethnographic methods to analyse global issues
  4. Contextualise and deconstruct visual media content, popular culture, and discourses through an intersectional analysis of race, ethnicity, class, gender, sexuality, and disability
  5. Design and execute an original research project, and revise project based on peer review on project proposal
