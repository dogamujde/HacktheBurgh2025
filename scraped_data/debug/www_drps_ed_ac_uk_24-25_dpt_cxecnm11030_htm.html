Postgraduate Course: Development Economics (ECNM11030)

Course Outline
SchoolSchool of Economics CollegeCollege of Arts, Humanities and Social Sciences
Credit level (Normal year taken)SCQF Level 11 (Postgraduate) AvailabilityAvailable to all students
SCQF Credits10 ECTS Credits5
SummaryThe course aims to provide students with an introduction to recent research in development economics.

Students will be exposed to a blend of theoretical models and empirical applications/results enabling them to get a better understanding of the obstacles to economic development. Most topics are focusing on the impacts of policies at the household level (micro). The last two topics look at macro topics with country/district/state level variables and impacts.
Course description The topics that will be covered are:

-Intra-household decision process
'As a first step we aim at looking at how a household makes decisions and how the the intra-household allocation of income and resources is made. This will help us understanding how decisions are made with respect to savings, insurance and investments in subsequent lectures.'
-Insurance
-Informal savings
-Introduction to randomized controlled trials
-Formal banking: Microfinance institutions (MFI)/Bank accounts
-Mobile Money
-Impact of Microcredit
-Education: Keeping children at school and improving learning outcomes
-Gender (female) empowerment
-Foreign Aid and Governance; Foreign Aid and Growth
